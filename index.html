üßò‚Äç‚ôÄÔ∏è Pigeon Pose

1-2 min hold each side

üí° Benefits

Opens hips, releases lower back tension, stretches piriformis muscle (major cause of lower back pain).

üéØ How To Do It

Setup
  • Begin on all fours (hands and knees)
Movement
  • Bring one knee up towards your arms and rest the outside of that leg on the ground
  • Extend your other leg straight behind you
  • Bring your trunk forward with arms straight on the ground until you feel a stretch
  • Hold for 1-2 minutes, breathing deeply
  • Repeat on the other side
Tips
  • Perform this exercise slowly and keep your back straight
  • Don't force the stretch - go only as far as comfortable
  • Focus on deep, relaxed breathing

‚ú® Kate's Tips

This is one of the BEST hip openers. Tight hips are often the root cause of lower back pain. Do this daily if possible!

üîÑ Sidelying Open Book Thoracic Rotation

10 reps each side

üí° Benefits

Improves mid-back mobility, opens chest and shoulders, relieves upper back stiffness from sitting.

üéØ How To Do It

Setup
  • Lie on your side with bottom leg straight
  • Top leg bent at 90 degrees, resting on a foam roll or rolled towel
  • Arms straight and together in front of you on the ground
Movement
  • Slowly move your top arm away from your other arm
  • Bring it to the floor behind you, rotating your upper trunk
  • Follow your hand with your eyes
  • Next, bring your arms back together and repeat
  • Do 10 reps, then switch sides
Tips
  • Move slowly and with control
  • Keep your bent knee stable on the foam roll
  • Focus on rotating from your mid-back, not your lower back

This one feels SO good after being hunched over a computer or phone. Your thoracic spine (mid-back) needs rotation to stay healthy!

ü¶µ Half Kneel Psoas Stretch

5-10 sec holds, 6-10 reps each side

üí° Benefits

Stretches hip flexors (psoas muscle), relieves lower back tension from sitting, improves posture.

üéØ How To Do It

Setup
  • Begin in half kneel position on your right knee
  • Left foot forward and flat on the ground
  • Place a pillow under your knee for comfort if needed
Movement
  • Tuck your pelvis under and transfer weight forward onto the left foot
  • Reach your right arm up overhead and slightly to the left
  • You should feel a stretch in the front of your right hip and thigh
  • This should NOT cause back pain
  • Hold 5-10 seconds
  • Do 6-10 reps, then repeat on opposite side
Tips
  • Keep your core engaged - don't let your back arch
  • The stretch should be felt in the front hip, not the back
  • Great to do after sitting all day!

‚ú® Kate's Tips

Tight hip flexors are a HUGE cause of lower back pain, especially if you sit a lot. This stretch is a game-changer! Do it every day.
